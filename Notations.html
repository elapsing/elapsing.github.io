Notations ​

Boldface lowercase letters, boldface uppercase letters, and lowercase letters denote vectors, matrices, and scalers, respectively.

Matrices and vectors are denoted by boldface upper-case and lower-case letters, respectively.

Calligraphy letters M are used to denote sets. And |M| represents the cardinality of the set M.

(⋅)T,(⋅)H denotes the transpose and the conjugate transpose.

The superscripts (⋅)T,(⋅)H and (⋅)† denote the matrix transpose, conjugate transpose and pseudo-inverse operations, respectively.

ℓp-norm of a vector and the expectation are denoted as ∥⋅∥p.

||⋅|| denotes the Euclidean 2-norm of a vector.

∥⋅∥ represents the l2-norm of a vector.

E{⋅}. Tr{⋅} represents the trace operation.

E{⋅} denotes statistical expectation.

⊗ denotes the Kronecker product.

AH and det[A] denote the conjugate transpose and determinant of matrix A, respectively.

IM denotes the M×M identity matrix.

CN(0,σ2) denotes the distribution of a circular symmetric complex Gaussian random variable with zero mean and variance σ2.
